1Problem statement & objective

Problem. Used-car buyers on Mudah don\'t have a clear signal whether a listing is fairly priced. Without that signal, buyers either spend time comparing listings manually, drop out because they\'re unsure, or contact sellers without confidence. This slows down how fast buyers act in the marketplace.

Hypothesis. Showing a price label (Competitive / Good / Fair Price) and a visual range bar on the adview page will give buyers more confidence to contact sellers. This should increase both the number of unique buyers who take action and the total leads sent.

Objective. Find out if Price Insight drives a measurable lift in buyer engagement on Mobile Web Cars For Sale.

What "lazy bucketing on eligibility" means

A user is not assigned to control or treatment when they first land on any Cars page. Instead, a user only enters the experiment when they first view a listing that has price insight data attached. After that, all their adviews are counted in the experiment — including views of listings without price insight data.

This is why the experiment only covers ~21% of MWeb Cars traffic: only ~21% of visited listings have price insight data, so only users who happen to land on one of those listings get pulled into the experiment.

Treatment surfaces

  • Badge — Label at the price section: Competitive Price, Good Price, or Fair Price. Only shown when the listing price is between the calculated lower and upper bounds (in-range).
  • Range bar — Below the description. Visual bar with a dot showing where the listing price sits compared to similar Mudah listings. Shows for both in-range and out-of-range listings.
  • Tooltip — A first-time nudge pointing to the range bar. Hidden if the badge is already visible above the fold, on repeat visits within 30 days, or after the user scrolls or opens the image gallery.

4How the data was analyzed

Aggregation approach

Daily counts are added together across the analysis window to get period totals. Two views are computed:

  • Aggregated view — period totals on the All segment (sum of all badge types per arm). This is the main view.
  • Cumulative trend — running total through each day, used to check for novelty effects.

Statistical tests

  • Two-proportion z-test for rate metrics (α = 0.05, two-sided)
  • Chi-squared test on user counts for SRM
  • 95% confidence intervals on absolute differences

Data scope adjustment

June 8 only had ~10% of master dashboard share vs a stable ~17–18% on every other day. This means the feature only went live partway through June 8. June 8 is excluded from the analysis to avoid skewing the early results.

6A/B test results

Period: 9 Jun – 25 Jun (17 days, June 8 excluded). SRM check borderline: control 121,201 vs treatment 122,338 users (χ²=5.31, p=0.021) — passes by convention but slightly imbalanced.

7Recommended next actions

Metric summary

All four metrics moved in the right direction but none are statistically significant yet. Leads per Adview (p=0.055) and Browser-to-Responder Conversion Rate (p=0.062) are closest to significance. The cumulative lift is still going down — from a +14% peak in week 1 to ~+3.7% by Jun 25.

Recommendation: wait 2 more weeks before deciding

  • The test is 17 of 42 days into the planned 6-week hold (~40% complete).
  • The lift is still decaying. We can\'t tell yet whether it settles at +3–4% (real effect) or keeps dropping toward 0% (novelty only).
  • Calling the test now risks shipping based on novelty, or killing a feature that has a small but real effect.
  • Next checkpoint: 6 Jul (4 weeks). If the lift flattens around +3–4% by then, we have a real (modest) effect. If it keeps falling, novelty was the whole story.
