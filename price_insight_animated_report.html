01
The Test

Problem & Hypothesis

Problem. Buyers browsing Cars for Sale listings on Mobile Web lack price context when evaluating a listing. Without a clear signal of whether a price is competitive, fair, or high relative to comparable cars, buyers are less confident acting on listings — even well-priced ones — leading to missed conversion opportunities.

Hypothesis. Surfacing a price label (Competitive / Good / Fair Price) and a visual price range bar on the adview page will guide users on where a listing's price sits, making their browsing more efficient and increasing how often they submit leads.

Control
Standard listing adview — price shown with no market context.
Treatment
Price label + range bar rendered below the description, showing where the listing sits vs comparable cars.
Treatment — first-time nudge
First-time viewers see a tooltip prompting them to check the price insight.

Summary — Hypothesis Status: Partially Confirmed

The hypothesis was that guiding users on where a listing's price sits would make browsing more efficient and increase how often they submit leads — a volume effect. That part is not yet confirmed: Leads per Adview (+2.04%, p=0.0523) and Leads per User (+1.72%, p=0.0941) are both flat and not statistically significant.

What the data does show clearly: the feature helps users act with more confidence once they've decided to reach out. Browser-to-Responder CVR is up +5.17% (p=0.0002) — highly significant and stable. Users who see price context are more likely to follow through and contact the seller, even if they aren't browsing or leading more overall.

Read together: this is a confidence effect, not yet a volume effect.
