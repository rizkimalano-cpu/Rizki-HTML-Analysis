Car Grant CTA — A/B Test Results

Adview Page · Design Redesign · 3 Platforms

N = 413,363 users 2-proportion z-test α = 0.05 (two-tailed) Hypothesis: new design ↑ adoption rate & CTR
Background
Context

Dealers on Platinum and Gold store tiers can display a Car Grant badge on the adview page. The Car Grant serves as tangible proof that the car listing is genuine, giving buyers more confidence to contact the seller.

Problem Statement

After launch, we observed that the Car Grant's adoption rate and click-through rate were lower than expected. This suggests buyers are not noticing or engaging with the feature, which limits its intended impact on lead generation.

Hypothesis

If buyers have a clear and prominent CTA to view the Car Grant, they will be more likely to interact with it, see the tangible proof of the listing's genuineness, and ultimately feel more confident reaching out to the seller.

What We Changed

We redesigned the Car Grant CTA on the adview page to make it more prominent and visually distinct — moving from a low-visibility treatment to a design that surfaces the trust signal more clearly to the buyer.

Design Changes
Control — Original Design Before
Characteristics
  • Outlined button style with "View Car Grant" label
  • Blue border with icon — blends into surrounding UI
  • Low visual weight, easy to overlook while scanning
  • No background fill — does not stand out as a trust signal
Treatment — New Design After
Characteristics
  • Full-width inline banner with light blue background fill
  • "Car Grant" label on the left, prominent "View" CTA on the right
  • Higher visual weight — clearly separates from listing content
  • Surfaces the trust signal more prominently within the adview flow

Shipping Recommendation
iOS
✓ Ship to 100%
The new design is a clear winner on iOS. Adoption rate jumped +79% and click-through rate jumped +72%, meaning significantly more buyers are noticing and engaging with the Car Grant. Crucially, leads per user also increased by +9.4% — so not only are more users clicking, they are also submitting more leads. There is no downside on any metric. Safe to roll out to 100%.
Mobile Web
✓ Ship to 100%
The new design meaningfully improved how buyers engage with the Car Grant — adoption rate up +24% and click-through rate up +12%. Lead volume stayed flat, which is a neutral result — the design did not hurt lead generation. Since the primary goal of this test was to fix low engagement with the feature, and that goal was achieved, this is a solid result. Safe to roll out to 100%.
Android
⛔ Do Not Ship
While the new design also lifted adoption rate (+65%) and click-through rate (+55%) on Android, we found a statistically significant drop in leads per user of −4.1%. This means treated users submitted fewer leads than the control group. In simple terms: the design is getting more taps on the Car Grant button, but fewer of those users are going on to contact the seller. That is a meaningful trade-off we should not ignore before rolling out to 100%.
