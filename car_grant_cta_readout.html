01

Problem statement & objective

Problem. The Car Grant label's CTR and adoption rate are low today. This matters because the label gives users tangible proof of car financing/grant information that can support their purchase decision — low engagement means most users never see it.

Hypothesis. Making the label appear more clickable — using a CTA-style treatment and an increased tap range — will increase both CTR and adoption rate.

Objective. Validate whether the redesign drives incremental CTA engagement without harming downstream lead conversion.

Why clicker-level conversion looks lower — composition, not quality

The redesigned CTA is far more prominent and easier to tap, drawing in a much larger, more casual clicker group than the old standalone button. This bigger group is, on average, lower-intent — which pulls the average conversion rate down even as the feature generates more total leads than before.

Recommended next actions

CTA engagement is up substantially and significantly across all three platforms — the redesign's core goal is clearly met. Both guardrails (leads/user, leads/browser) pass cleanly on all platforms — no significant decrease anywhere. iOS backs the engagement gains with a real, stable incremental lead lift (~+5%, significant). Android's early negative signal has washed out and stabilized near flat. Mobile Web is flat on funnel metrics but not negative. The lower per-clicker conversion rate reflects a larger, more casual clicker base, not a quality decline, and total leads from clickers are up on every platform.

Ship it

  • CTA engagement (CTR, adoption) is up substantially and significantly on all three platforms.
  • Both guardrails pass cleanly — no statistically significant decrease in leads/user or leads/browser anywhere.
  • iOS shows a real, stable incremental lead lift (~+5%, significant) sustained across the full period.
  • Android's early negative signal on leads-per-user has washed out and stabilized near flat.
  • Per-clicker conversion is diluted by a much larger, more casual clicker base — but total leads from clickers are up 4–51% across all platforms, and this doesn't touch the defined guardrails.
  • Caveat: the persistent SRM across all platforms (flagged in the earlier Car Grant analysis) should still be logged as an open item for the experimentation infra team, independent of this ship decision.
  • Next checkpoint: 2 weeks post-rollout (~Jul 29, 2026) — confirm guardrails still hold at 100% traffic and monitor for any delayed shift in leads/user or leads/browser.
