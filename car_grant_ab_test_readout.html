1Problem statement & objective

Problem. The original Car Grant CTA on the adview page — a standalone outlined button — takes up dedicated vertical space and visually competes with the primary conversion actions (Call, WhatsApp). We wanted to test whether a lighter, more integrated presentation could improve grant discoverability without harming lead conversion.

Hypothesis. Redesigning the Car Grant CTA into an inline banner-style row with a "View" action on the right will increase Car Grant CTA taps by reducing visual clutter, without harming lead-generation metrics (Call / WhatsApp taps and total leads).

Objective. Confirm that the new design (a) lifts Car Grant CTA engagement significantly and (b) does not cause a statistically significant regression in leads per user or leads per adview on any platform.

Note on eligibility and lead attribution

Because bucketing only happens when a user first lands on an eligible listing, treatment-bucketed users may subsequently visit non-eligible listings within the same session and submit leads there. Those leads are still attributed to the treatment group. This means the top-line lead numbers include both eligible-listing leads and non-eligible-listing leads for bucketed users. A follow-up BI cut (requested separately) will isolate eligible-listing leads only.

4How the data was analyzed

Aggregation approach

Daily counts are added together across the analysis window to get period totals. Two views are computed:

  • Aggregated view — period totals per arm, compared via two-proportion z-tests. This is the main view.
  • Cumulative trend — running total through each day, used to check for novelty effects.

Statistical tests

  • Two-proportion z-test for rate metrics (α = 0.05, two-sided)
  • Chi-squared test on user counts for SRM
  • 95% confidence intervals on absolute differences

Data scope adjustment

No data days were excluded — the 53-day window includes all valid experiment days from launch to readout. Rows with null experiment (organic non-bucketed traffic) were excluded from all rate calculations. Rows where experiment column is null (organic non-bucketed traffic) is excluded from the analysis.

7Recommended next actions

Metric summary

The primary metric (CTA CTR) is significantly positive on all three platforms with p-values well below 0.0001. On iOS, this translates into statistically significant lifts in leads/user (+4.2%, p=0.0007), leads/adview (+4.3%, p=0.0006), and leads/responder (+3.3%, p=0.009). On Mobile Web and Android, the CTA engagement lift does not translate into a statistically significant lead lift — but critically, no lead metric is significantly negative either. All 95% confidence intervals on Mobile Web and Android leads/user tightly bracket zero, indicating the sample size is well-powered to detect a meaningful effect. Guardrail metrics (user→responder rate, total leads normalised for split) show no regression on any platform.

Recommendation: Ship to 100% on all three platforms

  • iOS is a data-driven ship: 4 of 5 metrics significantly positive, including both primary and lead-related metrics. Estimated impact of +547 leads over 53 days on a normalised basis.
  • Mobile Web and Android show significant CTA engagement gains with no measurable harm to lead metrics (all lead-related p-values > 0.23, 95% CIs bracket zero). Shipping captures the CTA discoverability benefit at no measurable business cost.
  • The SRM is structural and platform-agnostic. It should be fixed before the next experiment, but does not change the interpretation of these results — the effect sizes on CTA CTR are far too large to be explained by a 2-percentage-point split imbalance.
  • Next checkpoint: Post-launch monitoring (Jul 11). Post-launch monitoring plan: track daily leads/user and total leads for 2 weeks post-100%-rollout on each platform. If leads/user drops by more than 2% vs the 30-day pre-launch baseline (on any platform), pause and investigate.
  • BI follow-up queries in flight: (1) leads restricted to Car Grant-eligible listings only, and (2) grant tapper funnel decomposition. These will refine the Mobile Web and Android read but are not blockers for the rollout decision.
